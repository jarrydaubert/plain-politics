# Civic Explainer Calendar

Last updated: 2026-07-01

## Purpose

Recurring political events and traditions should become evergreen, source-backed explainers. These pages help beginners understand what is happening, give search engines durable content, and create natural moments to bring users back.

## Content Principle

Each explainer should answer:

1. What is this?
2. Why is it happening now?
3. What should a beginner watch for?
4. Which public sources prove the dates, process, and claims?
5. What changed since last time?
6. Which glossary terms does the user need?

## Source Rules

1. Use UK Parliament pages for parliamentary process and traditions.
2. Use Electoral Commission pages for voting and election administration.
3. Use GOV.UK or official department pages for government publications and deadlines.
4. Use source snapshots and excerpts before presenting dates, process claims, or summaries.
5. If the official date is not announced, say so rather than guessing.

## Product Shape

1. Each explainer has a plain-English summary, timeline, key terms, source links, and related pages.
2. Annual explainers update from `evergreen` to `live now` when a date is announced.
3. After the event, the page keeps a `what happened` section and links to source records.
4. Related glossary terms appear inline.
5. Related change-feed items appear when relevant.

## Launch Opportunity

When the site is live and credible, outreach to MPs, journalists, teachers, civic groups, and political researchers should point to:

1. The postcode-to-MP starter.
2. The glossary.
3. A handful of excellent evergreen explainers.
4. The public source methodology.
5. A corrections/contact route.

Outreach should be factual and non-partisan: `Here is a free source-backed civic information tool; please check your page and report corrections.`
